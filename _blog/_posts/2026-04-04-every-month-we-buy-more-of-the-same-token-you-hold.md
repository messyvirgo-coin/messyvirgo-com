Most projects pay their bills by quietly selling their token. The team stacks stablecoins, holders eat the sell pressure, and everyone pretends it is "just the market."

Messy Virgo does the opposite. Our running costs are a built-in DCA plan where the two co-founders end every month holding more MESSY, not less.

## What March Really Looked Like

March wasn't cheap. Our AI agents, data feeds, infrastructure, and collaboration tools totaled 980 USD in hard costs. Those invoices are charged to personal cards; somebody has to pay them.

Here is how we handled it:

- We moved 3,000,000 MESSY from a treasury Safe into the marketing wallet to earmark March's budget.
- The project then sold 1.5M MESSY to Franco for 525 USDC at prevailing market prices.
- From that, 490 USDC plus 1.4M MESSY (worth about 490 USD at the time, using the same market rate) went to Michael, who had already paid the full 980 USD in March invoices from his own accounts.

End result:

- Vendors were paid in clean fiat.
- The DAO did not have to dump tokens into external liquidity.
- Both co-founders finished the month holding more MESSY than they started with.

March's burn wasn't a reason to derisk. It was a reason to double down.

## Why That Matters If You Hold MESSY

This setup sends three simple signals:

- We bleed with you. If MESSY went to zero tomorrow, the biggest losers would be the two people who keep trading their own dollars for MESSY to pay boring bills.
- No stealth sell pressure. Operations are funded by founder inflows, not by quietly selling into the same liquidity you use. When tokens move, it is traceable and priced off the same market everyone sees.
- Conviction is real, not rhetorical. Month after month, we have to decide: keep more USDC, or keep more MESSY? March shows our answer in numbers, not slogans.

If you choose to hold MESSY, you are not backing a team farming fees while unloading on the community. You are sharing upside and downside with founders who are literally buying the same token, at the same prices, to keep building.

And as long as those invoices keep coming, so does our DCA.
